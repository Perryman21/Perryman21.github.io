Welcome to Taniti

Taniti is a small, tropical island in the Pacific. While the island has an area of less than 500 square miles, the terrain is varied and includes both sandy and rocky beaches, a small but safe harbor, lush tropical rainforests, and a mountainous interior that includes a small, active volcano. Taniti has an indigenous population of about 20,000. Until a recent increase in tourism, most the Tanitian economy was dominated by fishing or agriculture.

Culture

Taniti’s vibrant island traditions come alive through music, dance, and warm community celebrations that welcome every visitor like family.

Food

From beachside grills to rainforest cafés, Taniti serves bold tropical flavors and fresh island ingredients you won’t find anywhere else.

Activities

Adventure seekers can dive reefs, hike volcanic trails, or simply unwind on sun‑soaked shores all in a single unforgettable day.

Travel

Your journey to Taniti begins with a scenic flight over turquoise waters that hint at the paradise waiting below.

Lodging

Stay in cozy bungalows, luxury cliffside villas, or eco‑friendly rainforest retreats designed to immerse you in island serenity.

Transportation

Whether by shuttle, bike, or breezy coastal tram, getting around Taniti is effortless and full of stunning views.
